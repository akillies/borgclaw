# COLLECTIVE
### A Short Film — Concept Script
*Inspired by Captain Power and the Soldiers of the Future (1987)*
*Tone: Grimdark. Intimate. One woman. Her machines. The thing she builds.*

---

**FORMAT:** 90-second concept film / pitch video
**AESTHETIC:** Industrial. Low light. Practical effects. No CGI except the hive dashboard — which is real.
**SOUND:** Sparse score. Mechanical. The hum of machines under tension.

---

## ACT I — THE WORLD (0:00–0:20)

*COLD OPEN. A massive server room. Brutalist architecture. Every surface black glass and brushed steel.*

*The camera slowly pushes in on a logo: three interlocking circles. Clean. Corporate.*

**SUPER: CLOUD COLLECTIVE**
**SUPER: Est. 2031. 94% of global inference capacity.**

*Racks of blinking servers. All identical. All humming the same note.*

**NARRATOR (V.O.)**
Three providers control the compute.
One pricing committee.
Every prompt, logged.
Every output, retained.
Your intelligence — their asset.

*Cut to: a NOTIFICATION on a phone screen.*

> *Your API spending limit has been reached.*
> *Upgrade to Enterprise to continue.*

*A hand swipes it away.*

---

## ACT II — THE TECHNICIAN (0:20–0:55)

*A workshop. Cluttered. Real. An old Mac Mini. A gaming PC with a cracked side panel. A tower NUC in the corner. All covered in dust.*

*THE TECHNICIAN — late 30s, exhausted, precise — powers on each machine in turn. No ceremony. Like lighting fires.*

*She opens a terminal. Types a single command.*

```
curl -fsSL borgclaw.io/install | bash
```

*She hits enter.*

*The screen fills with text:*

```
◉━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━►

  BORGCLAW ASSIMILATOR v0.1
  Scanning hardware...

  CPU:     Apple M4 Pro
  RAM:     24GB unified
  GPU:     Metal / MLX capable
  Profile: mac-apple-silicon-24gb
  Role:    QUEEN

  Recommended: You. Orchestrating.
```

*She exhales slowly. Something like relief.*

**TECHNICIAN**
*(to herself)*
There you are.

*On the second machine, a WORKER node registers.*
*On the third machine, a SATELLITE node registers.*

*The Queen dashboard appears on a monitor:*

```
  NODES ONLINE: 3
  UNIFIED CAPACITY: 62GB
  MONTHLY COST: $0.00
  STATUS: ASSIMILATING
```

*She leans back. Watches the hive come alive.*

---

## ACT III — WHAT IT MEANS (0:55–1:20)

*MONTAGE — fast cuts. Each beat is tight.*

*— A TASK routes from her phone to the cluster. Inference runs locally. Returns in 3 seconds.*

*— An AGENT sends a draft email. Waits for her approval. She reads it. Approves it. It sends.*

*— The AUTORESEARCH LOOP runs. A better embedding model is found. A task appears: "Evaluate nomic-embed-v2. Replace if metric improves."*

*— The Cloud Collective dashboard appears on a screen in the background. Usage: zero. The graph is flat.*

*She smiles. Just barely.*

**NARRATOR (V.O.)**
They built the collective to own your compute.

*She closes the Cloud Collective tab.*

**NARRATOR (V.O.)**
You built one of your own.

---

## FINAL FRAME (1:20–1:30)

*The workshop. Three machines glowing. The hive dashboard on the wall-mounted monitor.*

*She's already moved on. Working. The hive working with her.*

**TITLE CARD — slow fade in:**

```
  BORGCLAW
```

**SUBTITLE:**

```
  YOUR MACHINES.  YOUR MODELS.  YOUR RULES.
```

*The hum of the machines.*

*Cut to black.*

## PRODUCTION NOTES

**Tone reference:** The first act of *Captain Power* — before the action, in the quiet moment where you understand what was lost and what the fight is actually for. Grimdark not through violence but through implication. The Collective is never seen attacking anyone. It doesn't have to.

**The Technician:** No backstory given. No exposition. The audience fills it in. She's anyone who has hit an API limit at a bad moment. Anyone who has watched their infrastructure bill climb while their work got more dependent on it.

**The machines:** They should look real. Used. Not cinematic prop computers — actual machines that someone actually runs. The authenticity is the point. This isn't a startup demo. It's a person building something in their workshop.

**The dashboard:** Real. The Queen dashboard running on localhost:9090. If you're filming this, run the actual stack.

**Music note:** Something like Cliff Martinez meets early Hans Zimmer. Electronic but organic. The sound of a machine learning to breathe.
